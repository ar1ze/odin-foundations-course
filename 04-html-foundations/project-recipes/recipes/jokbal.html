A glistening platter of jokbal presents tender, gelatinous pig trotters braised until falling-off-the-bone, lacquered in a sweet-savory soy-garlic glaze. Aromas of star anise, ginger, and toasted sesame mingle as the meat is thinly sliced and served with spicy ssamjang and crisp lettuce for wrapping.

Ingredients

  • Pig trotters (pig's feet)
  • Soy sauce
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Rock sugar (or brown sugar)

Steps

  1. Clean and blanch - Rinse the trotters, then blanch in boiling water briefly to remove impurities; drain and rinse.
  2. Prepare the braising liquid - Combine soy sauce, water, smashed garlic, sliced ginger, star anise, and rock sugar in a large pot.
  3. Braise slowly - Add the trotters to the liquid, bring to a simmer, cover, and braise gently for 2â€“3 hours until the meat is tender and gelatinous.
  4. Reduce the sauce - Remove the trotters, strain the braising liquid, then boil it down to a glossy, concentrated glaze; return briefly to coat the meat if desired.
  5. Slice and serve - Thinly slice the jokbal, arrange on a platter, and serve with lettuce, ssamjang, pickled radish, and toasted sesame seeds.
